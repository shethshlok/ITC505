INTRODUCTION

Exploring the heritage of the indigenous people in Australia fascinated me to an extent which led me to travel north and learn the history of the indigenous people from Japan who are known as Ainu. 'Ainu' means 'Humans' in the Japanese language. It was astonishing to know about their tattoo culture which has existed since more than centuries ago which is a distinct feature of their community and culture. There were a lot of good qualities to be learnt from their culture which not only the population of Japan can imbibe in them; but also the whole world. To gain all the information I visited Encyclopaedia Britannica and Stand.ie which increased my knowledge about the great legacy of the Ainu community.

Ainu were the original habitants of Japan and are dated around 10,000 years old. They were the residents of the Hokkaido, Sakhalin, and the Kuril Islands which are the parts of Japan (the rural part of Japan). Physically, the Ainu stand out distinctly from the Japanese as a separate ethnic group because of their appearance and physical differences like tattoos on their bodies (Video, YouTube).The Ainu were hunters, fisherman, farmers and gatherers who lived in harmony with nature and practiced these for their living. After being invaded by the children of the sun, they were enslaved, killed and driven from their lands in Japan.

ANIMISM: THEIR RELIGION

The religion the Ainu community practiced was commonly known as Animism. Animism as described by the anthropologists is 'a religious system organized around a belief that plants, animals, inanimate objects, or natural phenomena have a spiritual or supernatural element.' (Nelson 2017, 6, 14). Their most important ritual took several years and it involved capturing a bear cub that was raised as a member of the family over the years. Taking care and treating the bear well in life would ensure that after death its spirit would ensure the well-being of them. This was a distinct feature of their community (Park, 2018).

Their Culture

Tattooing, for some amazing reason, has been a practice since the immemorial ancient times for the Ainu community. It's part of humanity and may also be defined as common day practice for them. 'However, we've been doing this up to the present to adorn our bodies, to mark ourselves with our life stories, or to express a belief or a part of our creative souls.' (Minerva, 2017). Tattoos back then were not merely for body decoration, but a woman actually cannot marry if she's not yet tattooed which was a custom of the Ainu community. Their famous tattooing culture consisted of facial tattooing which was only practiced by women, which they used to believe that tattooing was generated from 'Ancestral Mother' who is the younger sister of God. (Minerva, 2017)

Ainu and the Aborginals of Australia

As an anthropology student it was heartbreaking to know about how both the tribes were subjected to extreme racism and harsh government laws in both their respective governments. The indigenous people of Australia, the Aboriginals were colonized and exploited in the year of 1788 whereas the indigenous people of Japan, the Ainu community were forbidden by the government from practicing their rituals and their lands were declared as public lands which resulted in them losing their identity, culture and heritage.

However there are a lot of similarities among both the tribes of the Aboriginals of Australia and the Ainu of Japan. The Aboriginals were not allowed to use their language after the colonization. (Korff, 2020). Whereas on the other hand, the Ainu people were restricted to the access to their language in 1899. Just like the Aboriginals were given harsh government laws, The assimilation by the Japanese government caused the Ainu to be considered as a dying ethnic group.

Although there are similarities, both the tribes stand out from each other with different experiences they had, because of the white supremacy in Australia, The Aboriginals were snatched from their children also known as 'Stolen Generation'. In Japan, The Ainu people in 1899 were labelled as 'former aborigines' which can be classified as how the western influence has indulged in name-giving rather than sticking by the names of what they are known as.

CULTURE OF ABORIGINALS

They also had different cultures, The Torres strait Islanders of Australia had huge influence on oceans, islands and aquatic life whereas, the culture of Ainu people had an influence of blood sacrifice and bear rituals. Ainu people also believed a lot in after life. This shows how the old history and the importance of indigenous people's values that really influence me in today's competitive world. Their culture, practices and tradition is something which can incite many solutions to the happening issues across the globe relating from healthcare to global peace.

The first historical or documented appearance of Ainu occurred in the twelfth century in the Yuan Dynasty of China. It concerned a record of the people living in the region of Sakhalin (Namikawa, 2004). The Ainu people of Japan frequently clashed with the Nivkh, an indegeneous group of Sakhalin. The Nivkh were subjected to Yuan Dynasty rule. Starting from 1308 till the 16th century, the rule of the Yuan Dynasty was under the Ainu people. During this period they were indulged in trade with the Pan-Japan Sea. Also, the trading remained active with the Ryukyu (currently Okinawa), Kamakura and Muromachi governments of Japan.

AINU PEOPLE IN THE EYES OF OTHER

The Ainu people are also described as the image of the devil (Oni) by Suwa Daimyojin Ekotoba. In 1356. During this time, Wajin also describes the Ainu group as the dominant ethnic group of Japan. He also inferred that they were found in the region of Matsumae and Hakodate (the southern part of Ezo or Ezo-chi, or what was eventually called Hokkaido). Around the early 15th century, the trade with the Wajin was more abusive and was under the control of the Ando family. Wajin's trade was exploiting the Ainu resources, It got all stable under the rule of the Matsumae Han (clan).

The unfair practices in the trade continued until there were revolts seen from the Ainu people side and then the government took some steps to stop it, Like the government implemented stricter policies in order to control the Ainu people in Ezo. Despite the fact that the Ainu never formed a unity as a nation, the Japanese government considered the Ainu to be a foreign group. The government established Ezo-chi Bugyo (governing magistracy) and made efforts to assimilate and Japanize the Ainu people, thus putting forth the claim, 'Where the Ainu (Japanese) live' is Japan.' (Mitsuharu, 3-4)

The year 2008 was historic for the Ainu, since the Japanese government, for the first time in history, recognized them as an indigenous people of Japan. However, despite the government's promise to create a new law to help the Ainu recover their status, regain their culture, and rebuild relationships between Ainu and non-Ainu people in Japan, the corresponding law has not yet been created as of January 2012. The Japanese government has been reluctant to discuss injustices attributable to laws, which oppressed Ainu for the purpose of political gain. This tacit denial of responsibility continues to hamper any forward progress of policies that could begin true restoration of Ainu wellbeing.
